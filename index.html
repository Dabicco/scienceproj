What is a Domestic Cat?

  • Scientific name: Felis catus
  • Small carnivorous mammal
  • Weighs between 8-10 pounds (3.6-4.5 kg) on average
  • Comes in various coat colors, patterns, and lengths
  • Has retractable claws, heightened senses, and a slender, flexible body

Evolutionary History of Cats

  • 25 million years ago: First true cats (Proailurus) appeared
  • 12 million years ago: Modern cat family Felidae emerged
  • 4.2 million years ago: Small cats (Felis genus) evolved
  • 200,000 years ago: Wildcats (Felis silvestris) appeared
  • 9,500-10,000 years ago: First evidence of cat domestication in Near East

According to the National Geographic, cats likely began their association with humans when early farmers stored grains that attracted mice, and wildcats followed their prey into human settlements. These cats proved valuable for controlling rodents, and humans gradually formed bonds with the more docile individuals.

Cat Adaptations!

Amazing body features that help cats survive

Retractable Claws

Cats can 'hide' (retract) their claws when not needed and bring them out for climbing or hunting. This is a survival adaptation that helps cats protect their claws and prevent injury.

Night Vision

Cats can see very well in low light because of a special eye layer called the tapetum lucidum! This layer reflects light back into the retina, increasing eye sensitivity and therefore allowing cats to see better in low light.

Whiskers

Whiskers help cats measure spaces and feel what's around them, even in the dark! They are also sensitive to touch and can help cats navigate their environment, mainly small spaces or in the dark.

Flexible Body

A flexible spine lets cats twist, turn, and jump with amazing balance. This is a survival adaptation that helps cats catch prey or escape predators.

Soft Paws

Soft paw pads help cats walk quietly and cushion their landings This helps cats avoid injury and makes them more stealthy hunters.

Rough Tongue

Cats have rough tongues that help them catch prey and clean their fur. The rough texture of their tongues helps them grip and hold onto flesh, and the barbs on their tongues help them remove fur or skin from their prey aswell as their own fur.

Behavioral Adaptations

Behavioral adaptations that help cats survive

Hunting

Cats stalk, pounce, and catch prey using skills they learn as kittens

Grooming

Cats clean themselves many times a day to stay clean and healthy

Nap Masters

Cats sleep up to 16 hours a day to save energy for hunting

Play Time

Cats play to practice their hunting skills and have fun

Purring

Cats purr when happy or hurt - it helps them heal and feel better

Fun Fact!

Cats are most active at dawn and dusk. This is when their wild ancestors would hunt!

Fascinating Cat Facts

Cats sleep for 12-16 hours a day, which means a 9-year-old cat has been awake for only 3 years of its life!

A cat can run at speeds of up to 30 mph (48 km/h) in short bursts.

Cats can jump up to 6 times their length in a single bound.

A cat's purr vibrates at a frequency of 25 to 150 hertz, which can promote healing and bone growth.

Threats to Domestic Cats

  • Health Issues: Common diseases include kidney disease, dental problems, and diabetes
  • Predators: Outdoor cats face threats from larger predators like coyotes and hawks
  • Traffic: Road accidents are a leading cause of death for outdoor cats
  • Environmental Toxins: Exposure to pesticides, antifreeze, and other chemicals
  • Overpopulation: Leads to resource competition and increased euthanasia rates

According to the American Veterinary Medical Association (AVMA), proper veterinary care, keeping cats indoors, and spaying/neutering can help address many of these threats and improve feline welfare.
